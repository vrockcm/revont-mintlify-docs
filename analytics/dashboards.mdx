Dashboards in Revont let you assemble the call metrics and team KPIs that matter most to your organization into a single, shareable view. Instead of navigating across multiple reports, you can build purpose-built dashboards for different audiences — a weekly rep performance summary for your 1:1s, a high-level coaching health view for your VP, or a focused objection-tracking board for your enablement team — and keep everyone looking at the same numbers.

## Creating a dashboard

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Dashboards">
    Go to **Analytics → Dashboards → New Dashboard**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Name your dashboard">
    Give the dashboard a clear name and an optional description so teammates understand its purpose at a glance.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add your first widget">
    Click **Add Widget** to open the widget configuration panel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a metric type">
    Select the type of metric you want to track:
    - **Call volume** — Total calls logged over a period
    - **Coaching score** — Playbook adherence scores across reps or groups
    - **Talk ratio** — Rep-to-prospect speaking balance
    - **Action items completed** — Follow-through on post-call tasks
    - **Playbook coverage** — How consistently topics are being addressed
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure and save the widget">
    Set your preferred filters — date range, specific reps, or groups — then click **Save Widget**. The widget appears on your dashboard canvas.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Publish the dashboard">
    Repeat the process for any additional widgets you want to include, arrange them on the canvas to your liking, and click **Publish Dashboard** to make it live.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Widget types

Each widget you add to a dashboard serves a different purpose. Choose the type that best fits how you want to present the data.

| Widget type | Best used for |
|---|---|
| **Number card** | A single, prominent KPI — for example, average coaching score this week |
| **Bar chart** | Comparing reps or groups against each other across a single metric |
| **Line chart** | Showing how a metric changes over a defined time range |
| **Leaderboard** | A ranked list of reps ordered by a metric you choose |
| **Call list** | A filtered, scrollable list of calls that match criteria you define |

You can add multiple widgets of the same type if you want to track the same metric across different rep groups or time windows side by side.

## Sharing dashboards

Once a dashboard is published, click **Share** to control who can see it and how.

You have three sharing options:

- **Share with specific teammates** — Enter their email addresses to give named individuals access. They'll receive a notification and can view the dashboard when logged into Revont.
- **Generate a shareable link** — Creates a read-only link that anyone can open without logging in. Useful for sharing a snapshot with stakeholders outside your Revont workspace.
- **Embed in an external tool** — Copy the provided embed code to display the dashboard inside another platform, such as a sales wiki, internal portal, or BI tool.

Shareable links and embed views are always read-only — recipients cannot edit the dashboard or access any underlying call recordings.

## Scheduled exports

If you want your team to receive a regular summary without having to log in and check manually, you can automate dashboard delivery.

Click **Schedule** on any dashboard to configure a recurring export:

- Choose a cadence: **daily**, **weekly**, or **monthly**
- Select the recipients — yourself, specific teammates, or a distribution list
- Snapshots are delivered as **PDFs** attached to an automated email

Scheduled exports are a practical way to keep leadership or cross-functional stakeholders in the loop on call performance without requiring them to access Revont directly.

<Note>
  Dashboards are workspace-wide by default. Any **Admin** or **Manager** can view all published dashboards. **Reps** can only see dashboards that have been explicitly shared with them.
</Note>
